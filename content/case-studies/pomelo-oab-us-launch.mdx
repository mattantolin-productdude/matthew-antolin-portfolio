## Outcome

Launched online appointment booking for Pomelo's vaccine platform in the US, supporting county health departments and urgent care settings across multiple states. The HIPAA-compliant content governance system performed as designed — zero PHI exposure events post-launch and zero accounts requiring engineering involvement to modify content templates after go-live. The biggest product lesson: communications design in a regulated, privacy-sensitive, multi-stakeholder clinical environment requires the same discipline as engineering — explicit content governance, channel rules, and failure state handling designed before sprint one.

---

## Problem

Pomelo Health had a market-leading patient scheduling suite in Canada. The vaccine deployment expanded into the US, and the Canadian product had none of the required US compliance infrastructure: no HIPAA-regulated messaging architecture, no NPI-based provider lookup, no US timezone handling, and no insurance eligibility workflow. Building it wasn't just technical work — it was communication design work.

US vaccine deployments spanned county health departments (Berrien County MI, Brazoria County TX, East Central Health District GA, Greater Baden Medical Services MD, Outreach Health Services MS) and urgent care settings (Exer Urgent Care CA, CareCube NY, Pelvic Laser Institute FL, Ozlem Goker-Alpan VA, Sweeny Community Hospital TX). Each context had different regulatory posture and operational requirements.

---

## Constraints

**Regulatory.** Patient communications are HIPAA-regulated. PHI cannot appear in notification previews. SMS and email reminders required explicit opt-in capture at booking, channel-specific PHI handling rules, and audit-trail support. Clinic-level content governance requirements varied by account.

**Technical.** The Canadian OAB product had no concept of US timezone handling, NPI-based provider lookup, or HIPAA-aligned data handling across booking and reminder flows. The Practice Fusion EHR integration required a new integration layer so appointment changes in the EHR would cancel or update reminders without requiring front-desk intervention.

**Organizational.** Small product team. The Practice Fusion integration consumed most engineering capacity for the initial launch window. That constraint forced hard prioritization: one EHR and clear deployment sequencing before broadening.

**Operational.** US clinic and health department IT teams controlled reminder content governance. The product needed a template governance workflow — not just a template editor — to be deployable at scale without ongoing PM involvement in every account's content cycle.

---

## Role & Scope

I was the PM of record for this launch, owning the product from discovery through go-live across booking, intake, and notification flows. That included: leading structured discovery with clinic administrators, front-desk staff, and IT decision-makers; authoring US-specific functional annotation documents for each changed flow; writing the partner API requirements document for the Practice Fusion EHR integration; running UX review cycles with patients and schedulers; and producing clinic onboarding materials. I owned sprint-level prioritization and requirements, and ran clinic-side acceptance testing before each pilot go-live.

---

## Approach

**Targeted a winnable first segment.** Rather than supporting all US clinic types, v1 scoped to vaccine deployment contexts — county health departments and urgent care — on a single target EHR (Practice Fusion). One integration, one deployment pattern to replicate.

**Journey mapping before wireframes.** Mapped the full US patient journey from appointment search through post-vaccination confirmation, with explicit swimlanes for what Pomelo owns, what the EHR owns, and what the clinic owns.

![US Patient Journey, workflow scope across Pomelo, EHR, and clinic systems](/artifacts/pomelo-oab-us-launch/us-patient-journey.png)

**Functional annotations for every changed flow.** NPI-based provider lookup, US timezone handling, and HIPAA-aligned data handling across booking and reminder flows required functional annotation documents for each change — specifying exact message content, channel rules, error states, and retry behavior — before engineering started.

![Patient Booking UX Flow, full screen-level flow from search through confirmation](/artifacts/pomelo-oab-us-launch/oab-user-flow.png)

**Message strategy with explicit failure states.** The reminder flow covered:

- Channel selection (SMS vs. email) based on patient opt-in and PHI sensitivity
- Content inclusion without exposing PHI in notification previews
- Patient appointment changes after a reminder has already been sent
- Missing opt-in at booking
- Appointment cancellation timing issues

**HIPAA-compliant content governance.** Designed a configurable template system with a Pomelo-controlled base template and a clinic-level content layer. Clinics could modify their content layer without engineering involvement. A review workflow was built into the configuration process for IT team approval.

![Patient Journey Map, phases, touchpoints, and emotional arc from booking through post-visit](/artifacts/pomelo-oab-us-launch/patient-journey.png)

**Go-to-market enablement.** Produced US clinic and health department onboarding guides, front-desk training materials, and sales enablement collateral.

---

## Tradeoffs & Edge Cases

**Content governance vs. deployment speed.** Shipped with Pomelo-default templates and allowed customization after go-live, with a documented content freeze until IT completes their review.

**PHI in notification previews.** Message templates were designed to lead with non-PHI content and gate PHI behind an authenticated link — a content design constraint documented so clinic-customizable fields couldn't inadvertently introduce a HIPAA exposure.

**Timezone handling.** Appointment timezone must be stored with the event in the EHR integration contract, not inferred from clinic address — critical for accounts spanning multiple time zones.

---

## What I'd Do Next

**Earlier discovery with IT stakeholders on content governance.** US clinic and health department IT teams wanted more control over reminder content than we anticipated. IT-specific discovery before functional annotation work would have surfaced this earlier.

**Per-reminder performance analytics before expanding the reminder product.** Limited visibility into which cadences and content patterns drove the best outcomes. Adding per-reminder analytics at v1 would have created a feedback loop we didn't have until later.

**A HIPAA-compliant template library for common US deployment types.** A curated library for common vaccine deployment contexts would reduce the IT review burden and shorten onboarding timelines for standard profiles.
