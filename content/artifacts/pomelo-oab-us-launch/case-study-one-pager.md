# Case Study: Online Appointment Booking — US Market Launch
**Pomelo Health | Product Manager | 2021–2022**

---

## Opportunity
Pomelo's Canadian scheduling platform had demonstrated strong product-market fit in provincial clinic networks. US specialty clinics faced the same core problem — manual scheduling, high no-show rates, and meaningful daily revenue loss — with the added complexity of insurance eligibility workflows and a fragmented EHR landscape. The commercial thesis: win the US market with automated reminders as the low-friction entry point, then expand to full online appointment booking as integration depth and trust grew.

## Problem
US specialty clinics were losing meaningful daily revenue to no-shows and spending significant front-desk time on manual phone scheduling. Existing tools either lacked patient-facing UX or couldn't integrate deeply enough with clinic EHRs to change actual workflows.

## My Role
Sole PM for the US market launch. Responsible for discovery, product scope definition, partner API specification, functional design, UX research coordination, and go-to-market enablement.

## What I Did

| Area | Actions |
|------|---------|
| Discovery | Structured interviews with 12+ stakeholders — clinic admins, front-desk staff, IT leads |
| Scoping | Defined target segment (independent specialty clinics on a single target EHR) to constrain integration scope |
| Integration | Authored partner API requirements; led technical alignment with the integration partner |
| Product | Functional annotations for several US-specific flow changes; sprint reviews |
| UX research | Patient-side and scheduler-side expert evaluations; deprioritized polish, focused on flow friction |
| GTM | Onboarding guides, training materials, sales enablement collateral; defined pilot success criteria |

## Results
- **Multiple US clinic groups live** within the first two quarters of launch
- **Measurable no-show reduction** within 90 days across pilot accounts; specific figures confidential, available on request
- **Lead EHR integration shipped and live** — enabling bi-directional appointment sync and eliminating manual front-desk reconciliation
- Reminder activation rate exceeded internal targets; anchored the US sales motion through end of year

## Key Decisions
1. **Launched reminders before full OAB** — fastest ROI proof point, lowest clinic workflow disruption
2. **Targeted a single EHR in v1** — constrained integration scope to ship faster and generate a replicable success story
3. **Sequenced insurance eligibility prompting as a booking gate, not a post-booking step** — reduced downstream no-shows tied to coverage issues and gave clinic ops teams confidence in confirmed appointments
