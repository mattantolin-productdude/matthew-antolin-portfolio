# Case Study: Piper — Conversational Analytics Agent for Pharma Brand Teams
**Swoop | Product Manager | 2024**

---

## Opportunity
Pharma manufacturers lacked real-time visibility into how their HCP and DTC website AI agents were performing. Monthly analytics required analyst hours to produce and arrived too late to inform in-cycle brand decisions. The opportunity: a self-service analytics agent that gave brand teams natural-language access to the data they needed, on demand.

## Problem
Brand and commercial teams managing pharma website AI agents couldn't answer basic performance questions — coverage rate, unresolved inquiry rate, topic distribution, desired outcome click-throughs — without routing requests through an analytics team. Monthly PPTX reports were assembled by hand, took significant analyst time, and were stale by the time they landed.

## My Role
PM for Piper. Responsible for product strategy, user discovery, PRD authorship across multiple feature tracks, AI model evaluation process, and cross-functional delivery with engineering, design, and client success.

## What I Did

| Area | Actions |
|------|---------|
| Discovery | Qualitative research with brand managers, commercial analytics leads, and medical affairs stakeholders across manufacturer accounts |
| Product architecture | Designed per-brand, per-agent-type data model (manufacturer → brand → DTC/HCP) |
| Conversational UI | Built Piper Data Analyst agent with curated conversation starters mapped to real brand team decisions |
| Reporting automation | Automated monthly PPTX report pipeline — eliminated manual analyst assembly for standard reviews |
| HVA CTAs | PRD for contextual high-value action CTA serving based on in-conversation intent signals |
| AI evaluation | Built multi-step agent version comparator for structured model change assessment (topic clustering, gap analysis, QA scoring, impact assessment) |

## Results
- Piper launched across multiple pharma brand clients with separate DTC and HCP agent instances per brand
- Monthly reporting cycle moved from multi-hour analyst effort to zero-touch automated delivery
- Brand teams moved from monthly cadence to on-demand self-service analytics
- Data Analyst agent saw repeat engagement from brand managers who had never directly accessed engagement data before
- HVA CTA feature opened a new product surface directly tied to client campaign optimization

## Key Decisions
1. **Led with conversational access, not dashboards** — brand managers needed follow-up questions, not static charts; a chat interface scoped to common decision points solved adoption
2. **Made conversation starters outcome-framed, not metric-framed** — "Is my agent covering the questions patients have?" outperformed "Show me coverage rate" in every usability session
3. **Built the agent version comparator before shipping model updates** — replaced ad hoc judgment calls with a repeatable, documented evaluation process across prompt sets, topic clusters, and QA test results
